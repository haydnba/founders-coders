Photography

I have been involved with photography and digital imaging for around 20 years.

  • I teach two short evening courses at the City of Wesminster College in paddington: Studio Photography (an introduction to studio lighting theory and practice) and Introduction to Photoshop.
  • I review books on photography and history of photography for the Burlington Magazine.
  • I have worked as both production and photographer's assistant on film and photo shoots and as a darkroom technician.

While I really loved working in the darkroom with black and white processing, I have developed a strong interest in digital imaging and I hope to learn more about image processing and computer vision as I develop my computing skills (see my codewars kata Bit Plane Return.

Philosophy etc.

After studying photography at college (BTEC) I took a degree in Art History at Goldsmiths College. At university I came into contact with philosophy and theory and began to focus almost exclusively on these fields. In order to remain involved in the subjects after my degree I began participating in a charitable project called The Philosophy Forum. This is a philosophical discussion group for Islington residents with mental health problems (organised by the Stuart Low Trust). After attending as a discussion facilitator for 5 years I am now the key organiser for this project, in which capacity I am tasked with:

  • Managing the project website and other digital resources
  • Maintaining a team of volunteer facilitators
  • Looking after our participants, all of whom are vulnerable adults
  • Giving presentations on philosophical topics etc.

I really love this work and we are currently expanding the project to work in other areas of london and with other charities.

I am a regular participant in and contributor to the philosophical research/discussion programme at the Performing Arts Forum in Northern France.

I teach a 10 week course in Art History at CWC and I taught the subject at Foundation Degree level at Hastings College of Art & Technology (2007-8).

Computing

I became interested in computing a few years ago when I was volunteering as a classroom and learning assistant for Morley College in Lambeth. I was assigned to a computing class for absolute beginners and the tutor advised me to look into taking some courses in programming. In particular, she pointed me in the direction of ELATT, where she had recently been learning web design. I took a L1. Award in computing (web development) and then the L2. Award and Dimploma the next year. Some key areas/projects covered include:

  • HTML, CSS, UX, UI
  • Basic JavaScript
  • Programming with PHP and MySQL:
    project was to design and implement a content management system from scratch.
  • Relational Database Theory
  • Wordpress
  • Photoshop, Dreamweaver etc.
  • Agile development

Subsequent to taking this course I have continued to practice programming and to work on projects with/for friends - most recently learning to program in python and use the Django framework.

Other Interests

For the last 6 years my partner and I have managed the kitchen at Shelter From The Storm on Tuesday nights. SFTS is the only night shelter in London that is open year-round. My friends and I use our long experience working in catering and the service industry making sure that the 40-50 homeless guests that stay there each night are well fed. Normally it is extremely fun, especially compared to cooking and serving professionally.

I also work a lot as a handyman, decorator, gardener and as an assistant to a tree surgeon. I really enjoy practical work, making things, and trees...
