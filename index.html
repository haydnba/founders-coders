Photography

I have been involved with photography and digital imaging for around 20 years.

  • I teach two short evening courses at the City of Wesminster College in paddington: Studio Photography (an introduction to studio lighting theory and practice) and Introduction to Photoshop.

    I completed an Award (C&G) in Education and Training with CWC last summer.

  • I review books on photography and history of photography for the Burlington Magazine.

  • I regularly work as a production assistant on film and photo shoots and I used to work as a photographer's assistant and darkroom technician.

While I really loved working in the darkroom with black and white processing, I have developed a strong interest in digital imaging and I hope to learn more about image processing and computer vision as I develop my computing skills (see my codewars kata Bit Plan Return.

Philosophy, Art History etc.

After studying photography at college (BTEC) I took a degree in Art History at Goldsmiths College. Through my reading in the history of art I came into contact with philosophy and theory. After my degree I really wanted to stay involved in reading and discussing philosophy and theory and eventually I found a project that I could really get involved with: The Philosophy Forum. This is a philosophical discussion group for Islington residents with mental health problems. After attending as a discussion facilitator for 5 years I am now the main organiser for this project.

Food & Cooking

For the last 6 years I have managed the kitchen at Shelter From the Storm on Tuesday nights. SFTS is the only night shelter in London that is open year-round. My friends and I use our long experience working in catering and the service industry making sure that the 40-50 homeless guests that stay there each night are well fed. Normally it is extremely fun and definitely not as stressful as working in professional catering or service industry.

Trees & Gardens etc.

I have worked as an arborist's assistant for many years (Orange Trees), managing the situation on the ground and working with hand-tools in the trees. And through this work I have taken on a number of clients around London for whom I work as a gardener.

Computing

I became interested in computing a few years ago when I was volunteering as a classroom and learning assistant for Morley College in Lambeth. I was assigned to a computing class for absolute beginners and the tutor advised me to look into taking some courses in programming. In particular, she pointed me in the direction of ELATT, where she had recently been learning web design. I took a L1. Award in computing (web development) and then the L2. Award and Dimploma the next year. Some key areas/projects covered include:

  • HTML, CSS, UX, UI
  • Basic JavaScript
  • Programming with PHP and MySQL:
    project was to design and implement a content management system from scratch.
  • Relational Database Theory
  • Wordpress
  • Photoshop, Dreamweaver etc.
  • Agile development

Subsequent to taking this course I have continued to practice my programming and to work on projects with/for friends - most recently learning to program in python and use the Django framework.
